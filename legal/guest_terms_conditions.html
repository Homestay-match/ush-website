1Overview of USH Services

The USH Website connects local Hosts who provide home accommodations with students and professionals (Guests) seeking housing. When using the service, you enter into agreements with both USH and the applicable Host upon booking.

USH verifies every accommodation before inclusion in the service. However, USH does not act as an insurer of your experience as a Guest at the Accommodation.

USH uses reasonable efforts to verify Host identity and background based on available resources. The service disclaims responsibility for any Host background, history, or personal information accuracy. Individual Guest experiences may vary from posted representations.

References to Hosts being "verified," "approved," or "checked" indicate completion of verification processes only. These designations do not constitute endorsements or guarantees regarding trustworthiness, safety, or suitability.

USH cannot guarantee stable availability of accommodations, as it relies on Hosts to maintain current information.

Key Definitions

Accommodation(s): Private or shared furnished bedrooms inside a Host's home or guest house at the property. Accommodations may be spare rooms in apartments, houses, condos, townhouses, or other housing types accepted by USH.

Homestay Application: Request for accommodation with a Host through USH Services.

Match: Confirmed accommodation with a selected Host for a designated period.

Content: USH Content and User Content combined.

Dashboard: Profile options and services available to Guests and Hosts on the USH Website.

Guest: Person requesting accommodation through USH Services. Guests may be international students, American citizens, U.S. residents, interns, or professionals.

USH Content: Content that USH makes available, including proprietary content and licensed third-party content, excluding User Content.

Host: Person offering accommodation through the USH Website.

User: Guests, Hosts, and any other USH Website user.

User Content: Information, descriptions, reviews, photographs, applications, notifications, messages, or emails posted on the USH Website by users.

You/Your/Guest(s): Refers to the user or any person permitted to use the USH Website and Services on your behalf.

We/Us/Our: Refers to USH or any agent, designee, or contractor.

5Application Process

You may submit a Homestay Application through the USH Website to request accommodation with registered Hosts. You are bound by these Terms and Conditions upon submission.

To send a Homestay Application, you must register through the USH Website. You will need to pay an Application Fee and Security Deposit before USH staff begins searching for accommodation.

Fees can be found by clicking "Prices and Schools" on the USH Website. Security Deposit and Application Fee are charged immediately upon submitting your online Homestay Application.

Only upon successful submission of the application and necessary fees will USH staff begin the accommodation search.

You must provide basic information on your Homestay Application, such as gender, smoking status, school or physical address, requested accommodation dates, number of Guests (only two Guests who know each other and travel together may share), housing preferences, English level, dietary restrictions, allergies, medications, and other information USH deems relevant.

You may register a USH account directly through the Site using your email address and password, or through third-party social networking sites such as Facebook, Pinterest, Twitter, Instagram, or Google. USH is not responsible for any SNS content.

Upon completing registration, you will receive an email confirming your registration.

Sign Up Requirements

You may register only one account with USH unless you receive written authorization. You may not transfer your account to another user. USH reserves the right to disable or suspend your account if you create more than one account, or if any information proves fraudulent, inaccurate, incorrect, incomplete, or if you violate these Terms and Conditions or any policies or requirements.

You are responsible for providing current, correct, full, and accurate information throughout registration and for keeping your information up to date throughout your stay.

You are responsible for keeping your information safe and secure and may not disclose account access credentials to another party. Inform USH immediately if you suspect unauthorized account use.

There may be security, transmission, or other Internet and online service risks, which you expressly assume. You are responsible for obtaining all necessary software, hardware, and equipment to use online services, including updated web browsers and appropriate encryption, antivirus, anti-spyware, and Internet security software.

USH will not knowingly collect or retain information submitted by children under 18. If USH learns that personally identifying information from a child under 18 was unknowingly collected, it will immediately take all reasonable measures to delete the information from all computer and data systems.

Students returning to USH less than 6 months from their move-out date needing a new homestay will be charged half the application fee. Students returning more than 6 months from their move-out date pay the full application fee. The application fee is waived only for students returning within 6 months to the same homestay they previously occupied.

6a. Host and Match Responsibilities

Hosts, not USH, are fully responsible for accepting Homestay Applications and making accommodations available. Upon Match confirmation by the Host, you will be required to enter into an agreement with the Host through the USH Website and comply with any terms, conditions, rules, and restrictions imposed by the Host or USH.

You, and not USH, are responsible for fulfilling agreement obligations. USH is not a party to or insurer of such agreements.

6b. USH Cancellation and Refund Rights

USH reserves the right, in its sole discretion, to cancel or refuse service, applications, or Matches made via USH Services for any reason. USH may also refund part or all amounts charged to Guests in accordance with Section 7 and the Guest Cancellation Policy.

6c. Accommodation Selection

USH will select one accommodation based on location, booking dates, meal plan preferences, and availability.

You will receive an email confirmation once your homestay match is confirmed, requiring you to submit Housing Fees for the accommodation. You must also submit documents as part of the verification process and provide arrival information.

Only upon successful payment, document upload, and arrival information submission will USH release homestay information in a Welcome Packet. Guests may only visit the homestay and meet the host on the move-in date indicated in the Welcome Packet.

6d. Match Modifications

Hosts and Guests are solely responsible for requesting Match modifications. If you wish to make changes, you will request them as described in Section 7 and the Guest Cancellation Policy. Changes may include meal plan, occupancy, move-in and move-out dates.

6e. Information Accuracy

USH intends information, materials, and functions on the USH Website to be current and accurate but cannot guarantee or warrant accuracy or reliability. Actual and final fees are calculated by USH.

6f. Types of Fees

Application Fee: Administrative fee paid by the Guest to USH for each new Booking Request confirmation. This fee is nonrefundable and does not include extensions with the same Host, only new applications with different Hosts.

Security Deposit: Fee collected from each student, retained by USH until housing contract completion. The security deposit is nonrefundable in case of cancellation after homestay matching services begin. It can be used for outstanding telephone bills, damage, and/or cleaning costs. USH returns the security deposit after Host verification 30 days from departure. The deposit expires 36 months after move-out.

Housing Fee: Fee paid to the Host for the accommodation and plan selected. USH reserves the right to collect a percentage for administrative purposes.

Convenience Fee: A 5.80% convenience fee applies to all credit card payments. The fee may vary.

Taxes: Where applicable, USH collects required taxes.

Total Fee: Total of all above amounts charged when a Host accepts a Homestay Application.

Cancellation Fee: USH reserves the right to charge either $225 or 10% of the refund amount, whichever is greater. This fee is deducted from remaining housing fees for any cancellations, before or after move-in.

Wire Transfer Fee: Guests choosing wire transfer payment must contact USH for bank information. Wire transfers require a $55 bank fee, added to the amount due for each transfer.

Other Potential Fees

Airport Transfer Fee: Fee charged by USH for airport shuttle transfer to the accommodation. The Airport Shuttle Fee is not refundable once USH sends shuttle reservation confirmation. Do not pay the shuttle driver directly unless tipping. The Airport Transfer Fee will be added to your Total Fee and must be paid prior to arrival.

Late Fee: Fee a Guest may pay if payment is not received before the required due date. An additional U.S. $150.00 late fee is charged if your reservation is made less than 15 days prior to arrival.

Damage Preauthorization: At Homestay Application time, each Guest authorizes USH to charge their credit card up to $500 for Guest-caused damages. This preauthorization limit does not limit responsibility and liability for Damage Claims exceeding this amount.

6g. USH Legal Action Rights

USH has the right to pursue legal action for unpaid fees and other compensation when due.

6h. Booking Confirmation

This is subject to availability and placement determination, which is in USH's and Hosts' sole and absolute discretion.

6i. Move-in Confirmation

After moving into the accommodation, you will receive an email confirming your arrival. You have 5 days from arrival to contact USH with any questions or concerns about the accommodation or Host and to request accommodation changes.

If you don't contact USH via email about accommodation or Host problems within this period, USH will release payment to your Host.

6j. Failure to Move In

If you haven't moved in and fail to notify USH, please refer to Section 7 and the Guest Cancellation Policy.

6k. Fee and Price Changes

USH reserves the right to change fees and prices by posting updated information on the USH Website. Changes will not affect prior Homestay Applications and confirmed Matches.

6l. Rounding Off

All fees are received and paid in USD. USH may, in its sole discretion, round up or down amounts payable to the nearest whole functional base unit in which the currency is denominated.

Guests may cancel an unconfirmed Homestay Application or confirmed match at any time prior to arrival or after move-in. All cancellations must be sent in writing to the provided email address. Cancellation submitted solely to your Host will not be considered.

Your cancellation or a Host cancellation will be reviewed and determined by USH in accordance with the Guest Cancellation Policy. Any available refund will be promptly credited to your account or returned to the same credit card within 21 days.

You are responsible for informing USH if the credit card used is no longer valid. USH will not be responsible for any loss of funds due to an invalid card refund process.

7a. Misconduct Termination

USH reserves the right to terminate a Guest's Match without compensation if the Guest is found to have behaved with misconduct. Misconduct includes theft, abuse of property, verbal or physical abuse of persons, smoking inside the accommodation, alcohol or drug use, failure to adhere to federal, state, or local laws, school regulations, and USH rules and guidelines. No refunds whatsoever will be paid to Guests found to have behaved with misconduct.

7b. Homestay Change Policy

Guests may request changes to a Match through USH before or after move-in. Changes may refer to dates, meal plan, or occupancy. Any change request is subject to Host approval. If your Host accepts, the Match will be changed and your payment updated.

8Guest Services and Procedures

8a. Arrival and Departure Information

You are solely responsible for informing USH about all arrival, departure, and other timing matters pertaining to the accommodation, no less than 10 days in advance. Failure to do so may result in Match cancellation.

8b. Airport Shuttle Transfer

Guests are solely responsible for airport-to-accommodation transportation. Guests are not permitted to ask hosts for airport pickup or drop-off.

USH may, at its sole discretion, arrange transport to or from the airport with a third-party company for an Airport Transfer Fee. A minimum of 5 days is required for shuttle reservation consideration. The Airport Shuttle Fee is not refundable once USH sends shuttle reservation confirmation.

8c. Transportation

You are solely responsible for arranging and paying for your own transportation to and from campus and throughout the city where the accommodation is located. If you and your Host make any transportation agreement, those arrangements are solely between you and the Host. USH will be free of any liability in case of accidents, collisions, damage to properties, death, body injuries, or any involvement in such circumstances.

8d. Meal Plan Options

Hosts may offer one or more of the following meal plans. Your confirmed Meal Plan will be displayed on the Welcome Packet.

Room Only: No meals are offered. Guests purchase their own meals and snacks.

Room with Breakfast: Continental breakfast is offered by the Host, usually involving no cooking but including cereals, toast, milk, tea, coffee, fruit, pastry, or similar items.

Room with Breakfast and Dinner: Continental breakfast as described above, plus dinner prepared by the Host. The Host will serve the same dinner portion they have that night.

8e. Insurance

Guests are responsible for obtaining and maintaining their own insurance for accommodation-related matters, including property damage insurance for Guest-caused damages or personal property damage or loss.

8f. Extension

If you wish to continue your stay longer than your original Matching dates, you may request an extension. All extensions and payments must be directed through USH. Guests and Hosts may not contract directly with one another or reach agreements outside USH Services.

All extension payments must be received prior to the first extension period day. If payment is not received by that date, you agree to pay Late Fees of $85 plus an additional daily fee of up to two times the average daily rate.

8g. Overstaying without USH or Host Consent

Guests continuing to live in their accommodation past their move-out date without USH and Host consent are held responsible for paying the daily accommodation fee and any applicable fees. You accept that overstaying entitles the Host to make you leave immediately in a manner consistent with applicable law.

8h. Homestay Change Request

If unsatisfied with the Host or accommodation, you may request one accommodation change without new Booking Fee charge, provided you request reassignment within the first 5 days of arrival. Homestay Change requests must be made via email describing change reasons.

USH will review the request and may offer alternative options in your interest area. Changes are subject to another accommodation's availability, and USH does not represent that another arrangement can be made.

9Guest Privileges, Limitations and Obligations

Upon sending a Homestay Application, you agree to certain privileges and limitations that a Host and USH may offer. You are required to respect them according to these Terms and Conditions; otherwise, your Match may be cancelled and the Guest Cancellation Policy will apply.

9a. House Rules

Each Host is entitled to impose certain house rules at the accommodation. House rules may vary from accommodation to accommodation. Some basic house rules may be found in the Welcome Packet.

9b. Personal Privacy

Guests are entitled to personal privacy inside the accommodation. However, Hosts may occasionally inspect the Guest bedroom.

9c. Cleaning

Guests are responsible for maintaining the bedroom clean and organized at all times. Guests must also clean up after themselves in all other used areas.

9d. Privacy of Host's Personal Information

Guests must not share or distribute Host information to third parties such as name, address, phone number, email address, family details, or other information without Host consent.

9e. Key

The Guest will be provided with a key, keycard, or code to access accommodation premises by the Host. You are responsible for keeping the key, keycard, or code safe and returning it to the Host before move-out. If you lose or fail to return the Host's key/keycard, it will be considered a Damage Case, and you will pay applicable locksmith service costs.

9f. Laundry Access

Laundry facilities vary from accommodation to accommodation. Some accommodations offer free laundry, others have none, and some may have charges. If available, some Hosts may limit laundry use to once or twice weekly and establish a schedule. Laundry detergent is the Guest's responsibility.

9g. Kitchen Access

Guests should have access to some kitchen space to store food in the fridge and a Host-designated cabinet. Kitchen access varies from accommodation to accommodation.

Limited Kitchen Access: Hosts may allow Guest use of some appliances such as toaster, microwave, kettle; however, stove and oven use may not be permitted.

Full Kitchen Access: Guests should be allowed to use all offered kitchen appliances; however, certain Host kitchen rules may apply.

9h. Common Areas

Guests should have access to common areas such as living room and bathroom. However, certain areas such as the Host's bedroom or other rooms may be restricted from Guest access at the Host's sole discretion.
