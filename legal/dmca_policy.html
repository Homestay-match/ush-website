1Overview

Universal Student Housing Inc. ("USH") respects intellectual property rights and expects users of its website, applications, and services to do the same. The Site, Application and USH Services are collectively referred to as the "USH Website."

USH has a policy of terminating repeat infringers and may limit access or terminate accounts of users who infringe intellectual property rights of others.

2Notification of Alleged Copyright Infringement

To report copyright infringement on the USH Website, please submit written communication to our Designated DMCA Agent containing all of the following:

  1. Sufficient identification of the copyrighted work or intellectual property claimed to be infringed
  2. The URL or specific location on the USH Website containing the allegedly infringing material
  3. An electronic or physical signature of the copyright owner or their authorized representative
  4. A statement of good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law — and that the information provided is accurate, attested under penalty of perjury
  5. Your name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address

Important: You may be liable for damages, including court costs and attorney's fees, if you materially misrepresent that content on the USH Website is copyright infringing.

Send your notice to our Designated DMCA Agent:

USH Inc.
Attn: Designated DMCA Agent
Email: dmca@ushstudent.com

Upon receiving a proper notification, USH will remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material and notify the alleged infringer. Please be aware that personal information provided in your notice will be shared with the alleged infringer.

3Counter-Notification Procedure

If you believe that content was removed or disabled by mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notification letter containing all of the following:

  1. Identification of the removed or disabled material and its previous location on the USH Website
  2. The following three statements:
    • Consent to the jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for the judicial district in which your address is located
    • Acceptance of service of process from the party who submitted the original infringement notice
    • A good faith statement, under penalty of perjury, that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification
  3. Your name, address, telephone number, email address, and USH username
  4. Your physical or electronic signature

We will restore your removed or disabled material after 10 business days but no later than 14 business days from the date we receive your counter notification, unless the original complainant files court action to restrain the allegedly infringing activity.

4Retraction Procedure

If you are the original complainant and wish to retract a claim, you must provide a written statement including:

  1. The date of the original complaint
  2. A description of the content that was the subject of the original complaint
  3. The URL of the content on the USH Website
  4. Your physical or electronic signature

Please send retraction requests to dmca@ushstudent.com.
