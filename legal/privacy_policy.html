1Our Commitment to Your Privacy

USH Inc. ("USH", "we", "us" and "our") knows that you care how information about you is used and shared, and we appreciate your trust that we will do so carefully and sensibly.

This Privacy Policy covers personal data collected through www.ushhost.com and www.ushstudent.com, including mobile applications and all associated services. "You" or "your" means any visitor or user. Continued use of the USH Website indicates acceptance of any policy modifications.

2Scope of this Privacy Policy

This policy applies to the USH Website only. Links to other sites are not covered by this policy, and USH is not responsible for the privacy practices of external websites.

3Information We Collect

We collect information in two main ways — directly from you when you use our services, and automatically as you interact with our website.

Directly Provided

  • Name
  • Contact information (address, email, telephone)
  • Identifying information (passport, social security, driver's license)
  • Educational history and occupation
  • Birthdate, age, gender
  • Third-party references (with written consent)
  • Credit card and billing information
  • Financial account information
  • Other requested information

Automatically Collected

  • IP address
  • Phone number & device ID
  • Physical location
  • Browser and operating system type
  • Referring URLs
  • Cookies and pixel tag data
  • Website usage patterns

We may also collect information about you from publicly available websites, such as LinkedIn and Facebook, as well as information from consumer reporting or background check agencies or public records.

4Cookies & Other Technology

Cookies are text files storing alphanumeric identifiers that track website usage patterns and improve functionality. You may delete or block cookies, but doing so may limit certain site features.

"Web beacons" are small embedded images used to measure website usage and activity. Third-party service providers assist in analyzing activities without sharing personal user data directly.

We use cookies, pixel tags, and web beacons for personalization, fraud prevention, and service improvement purposes.

5How We Use Information

USH provides accommodation systems where hosts offer and guests/students request accommodations. We use the information we collect to provide and improve these services. Specific uses include:

  • Create profiles from provided or imported information
  • Obtain consumer reports and background checks
  • Provide personalized, targeted, location-based content and advertising
  • Enable user contact and invitations
  • Import/export information from other sites
  • Contact you regarding updates and security information
  • Inform you of other products or services
  • Provide your information to matching parties (hosts to guests/students and vice versa)
  • Generate internal usage reports
  • Provide public information to search engines
  • Detect and prevent policy violations or illegal activity
  • Make suggestions and inferences about your interests

Information you post in public areas of the USH Website or make visible to others may be accessed, used, and stored by others around the world, including those in countries that might not have legislation guaranteeing adequate protection of personal data.

6How We Share Information

USH does not share your personal information with third parties for their own direct marketing purposes without your affirmative agreement. We share information in the following categories:

Service Providers

Third-party service providers (web hosting, data analysis, marketing, payment processing, customer service, background checks) may access necessary information for specified functions only.

Guest / Host Information Disclosure

  • Accommodation information and host details appear in the database
  • A confirmed booking discloses guest name, contact details, and personal data to the host
  • Matching requests disclose host name, contact details, and personal data to guests

Aggregated Data

Aggregated data does not identify users individually and may be used by us for any business purposes and made available to third parties, including employers, job candidates, investors, and the public.

Legal Disclosure

Information may be disclosed when legally required or when USH believes action is necessary to meet legal requirements, protect rights or property, prevent crime, or protect public safety.

Business Transfer

In the event of an acquisition or sale of USH's business, information may be transferred to the acquiring party. Users will receive notification via email or a prominent notice on the website.

7How to Access, Update or Delete Your Personal Data

You may edit your account or request data changes by emailing support@ushstudent.com or calling +1 (310) 824-4908. We recommend periodically reviewing your data for accuracy.

Because many users use our services at different points of their student or hosting needs, we retain your personal data for continued service and convenience purposes until you advise us not to do so.

Data may also be retained for legal compliance, record-keeping, dispute resolution, fraud prevention, agreement enforcement, or other lawful purposes. Anonymized and aggregated data may be retained as needed.

8Account Security

The security of your USH Account relies on your protection of your password. You are solely responsible for all account activities and password protection. Never share your password with others.

USH will never request your password via email. Any request from a URL not beginning with https://www.ushhost.com or https://www.ushstudent.com should be treated as suspicious and reported to USH immediately.

Sharing your password grants third parties access to your account; you are responsible for all resulting actions. Report any unauthorized access to USH immediately.

9Security of the USH Website and Services

While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal data, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure.

We follow industry-standard protections. In the event of an inadvertent disclosure, we will take commercially reasonable remedial steps as required by applicable law.

10Children

The USH Services are not intended for, nor do we knowingly collect information from, children under the age of majority.

In compliance with COPPA, we do not collect personally identifiable information from users under 13 without verified parental consent. In compliance with GDPR, we do not collect information from users under 16 in the EEA without parental consent.

Children should not register or submit information to USH. If we become aware that data has been collected from a child without verified parental consent, we will delete it promptly. Please report any suspected child data collection to us immediately.

11International Visitors

USH operates US-based servers, meaning personal data is transferred to, stored, and processed in the United States. The United States does not have the same data protection laws as the EEA, Canada, and some other regions.

By providing your personal data to USH, you consent to its transfer to and use in the United States in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

12Specific Information for EU / EEA Users

Legal Basis

Processing of your personal data occurs based on your consent or steps taken at your request prior to entering into a contract.

Your Rights

  • Rectification: Correct inaccurate personal data
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your data under certain circumstances
  • Restriction: Restrict processing when accuracy is disputed or processing seems unlawful
  • Data Portability: Access your personal data and receive copies in structured, machine-readable formats transferable to other controllers
  • Complaint: Lodge a complaint with national data protection regulators for GDPR non-compliance

Consent Withdrawal

If you withdraw your consent, we will stop processing the relevant personal data except to the extent we have other grounds for processing under applicable laws. Withdrawal prevents future service provision but does not affect any prior lawful processing.

To exercise any of these rights, contact USH using the information in the Contact Us section below.

13How We Respond to Do Not Track Signals

There is no consensus among industry participants as to what "Do Not Track" means in this context. Like many websites and online services, we do not alter our practices when we receive a "Do Not Track" signal from a visitor's browser.

More information is available at www.allaboutdnt.com.

14California Residents — Shine the Light Law

California Civil Code Section 1798.83 permits California residents to request annual lists of personal data shared with third parties for direct marketing purposes, including names and addresses. Such requests are free and limited to once per year.

Under Section 1798.83, we currently do not share any personal data with third parties for their own direct marketing purposes.

Continued use of the USH Website after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

17Consent to SMS Notifications

By providing your phone number to USH, you grant Universal Student Housing permission to send you text messages related to your account and services. Message and data rates may apply.

We will only send one SMS as a reply to you, and you will be able to opt out at any time by replying "STOP".

No mobile information is shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing purposes. Text messaging consent data is not shared with any third parties.
