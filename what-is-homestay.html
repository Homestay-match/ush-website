What is Homestay?

Your Home Away From Home

Live with a welcoming family while you study in the U.S. Homestay is more than housing. It is a meaningful connection to American culture. It is the fastest way to improve your English, build lifelong relationships, and feel at home from day one.

The Basics

Living with a family, not in a dorm

Homestay means living in a private home with an American host family during your studies. Instead of a dormitory or apartment, you become part of a household. You will share meals, daily life, and a genuine human connection.

Your host family provides a private furnished bedroom, meals, and a supportive home environment. In return, you get something no dorm can offer. You will gain real daily practice in English, an inside look at American culture, and a family that actually looks out for you.

"Every match is made by a real person, not an algorithm. We take the time to understand both the student and the family before making a meaningful placement."

Who It's For

Is homestay right for you?

Homestay works especially well for students who want more than just a place to sleep.

English Learners

Daily conversation with your host family accelerates language skills faster than any classroom. You speak English from morning to night, in real situations.

First-Time Students Abroad

Moving to a new country alone is hard. A host family provides structure, safety, and someone to answer your questions — especially in those first confusing weeks.

Students Who Want a Home

If you want to feel settled — not just housed — homestay gives you a real home base with someone who genuinely cares about how your day went.

Budget-Conscious Students

Room, meals, utilities, and WiFi are all included. Compared to renting an apartment in Southern California, homestay is significantly more affordable.

Young & Underage Students

Many of our students are under 18. Host families provide a supervised, caring environment that gives parents back home genuine peace of mind.

Short or Long Programs

Whether you're here for 4 weeks or a full academic year, homestay placements are flexible. We match you with a family whose availability fits your schedule.

A Typical Day

What daily life looks like

7am
Morning with your host family

Breakfast together — a simple but meaningful daily ritual. Chat about the day ahead, practice your English in a relaxed setting, no pressure.

9am
Head to school or university

Many host families live within commuting distance of your campus. Your USH coordinator ensures the match makes geographic sense for your program.

3pm
Afternoons your way

Study in your room, explore the neighborhood, or join your host family on errands or activities. Some families invite students to weekend outings, parks, and local events.

7pm
Dinner together (Full Board)

Home-cooked dinner with your family. This is where real conversations happen — culture, food, stories. Students consistently say this is their favorite part of homestay.

9pm
Evening wind-down

Your private room is your space to decompress, study, video call family, or just rest. You have full privacy whenever you need it.

Common Questions

Homestay FAQs

What types of families can I be placed with?

USH host families come in all shapes and sizes — couples, families with children, single adults, and retirees. All live in houses, apartments, or condos across our service areas. What they all have in common: they've been personally verified by our team and genuinely enjoy hosting international students.

Can I request a specific type of host family?

Yes. You can share preferences on your application — such as whether you'd prefer a family with children, no pets, or a specific dietary setup. We can't guarantee every preference but we do our best to match thoughtfully. Every placement is made by a real person who reads your profile.

How are host families screened?

Every host family goes through our 98-point in-person home inspection. A USH team member physically visits the home, meets the family, inspects the student's room, and verifies safety and comfort standards. All adult residents complete a background check. We visit — no exceptions.

What if I'm not comfortable with my host family?

Contact our team and we'll work to resolve it. In most cases, a conversation is all it takes. If a rematch is needed, we handle it. Students are never left in a situation they're uncomfortable with — your wellbeing comes first.

What payments are required to book a homestay?

Booking typically requires an application fee, a security deposit, and advance payment for your first period of stay. We accept credit card, bank transfer (wire), and in some cases check or cash. All payment details are provided during your application process.

Can I stay for just a few weeks?

Yes. We offer placements from short-term programs (a few weeks) all the way through full academic years. Minimum stay requirements may apply depending on availability and program type. Let us know your dates when you apply.
